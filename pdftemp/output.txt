Insurance Claim Processing System             UCS3402 — Database Management Systems
1.  Introduction
The Insurance Claim Processing System (ICPS) is a large-scale enterprise system used
by insurance companies to manage policyholders, insurance policies, claim submissions,
verification processes, and final approval or rejection of claims.  The system ensures data
integrity, consistency, and efficient claim lifecycle management across multiple insurance
product lines including Medical, Vehicle, Property/Fire, Life Insurance, Natural Disaster,
and Theft policies.
This report documents the complete database design for ICPS across two assignments.
Assignment  1  covers  the  conceptual  design  using  an  Entity-Relationship  (ER)  model,
identification and justification of entities and relationships, and the ER diagram.  Assign-
ment 2 extends the design to a document-based NoSQL database (MongoDB), covering
NoSQL schema design, physical collection schemas, optimisation strategies, and CRUD
operations.
2.  Problem Statement
The objective of this project is to design a comprehensive database schema for the In-
surance Claim Processing System (ICPS). The system must support policyholder regis-
tration, policy management, claim submission, verification and approval workflows, and
reporting, while maintaining data integrity and minimising redundancy.
For Assignment 1, the design uses a traditional relational database modelling approach
with  an  ER  diagram.   For  Assignment  2,  the  design  is  extended  to  a  document-based
NoSQL database (MongoDB) to leverage schema flexibility, embedded document support,
and horizontal scalability.
The system must handle:
•  Multiple insurance product types through a flexible Event-based classification mech-
anism
•  Branch-level policy management
•  Third-party service provider tracking for claims
•  Complete claim status audit trail
•  Claim verification by authorised surveyors
•  Payment settlement for approved claims

4.  Identification of Entities, Attributes, and Relationships
4.1  Design Procedure Adopted
The database design for the ICPS was developed using a structured conceptual modelling
approach to ensure completeness, correctness, and real-world applicability.
Requirement Analysis
The problem statement was carefully analysed to extract key business objects and op-
erational  activities.   Important  nouns  such  as  policyholders,  policies,  branches,  events,
claims, service providers, surveyors, reviews, status histories, and payments were identified
as potential entities.  The interactions between these objects were studied to understand

Identification of Core Business Entities
Entities were selected based on the following criteria:
•  The object must have independent existence.
•  The object must store persistent data.
•  The object must have a unique identifier.
•  The object must participate in system transactions.
Identification of Attributes
Attributes were determined based on:
•  Information required to uniquely identify each entity.
•  Data necessary for verification, validation, and claim processing.
•  Attributes required for auditing and reporting.
•  Elimination of redundant or derived storage wherever possible.
Identification of Relationships
Relationships were derived from business rules such as:
•  A policyholder owns one or more policies.
•  A branch manages one or more policies.
•  An event categorises one or more policies.
•  A policy can have multiple claims.
•  A claim is handled by a service provider.
•  An event triggers a claim.
•  A claim has supporting documents.
•  A claim has a status history.
•  A surveyor reviews claims through a ClaimReview.
•  Approved claims are settled through payment.
Classification of Attribute Types
Attributes were classified as:
•  Simple attributes — atomic, indivisible values
•  Composite attributes — Address (consisting of street, city, state, pincode)
•  Multivalued attributes — Phone (a policyholder may have multiple contact num-
bers)
•  Derived attributes — Age (calculated from DOB), PolicyDuration (from Start-
Date and EndDate), ClaimProcessingTime (from submission to decision)

Constraint Identification
Participation constraints and cardinality ratios were assigned according to real-world in-
surance operations to ensure data consistency and integrity.  All entities in this design are
strong entities with their own primary keys.  This systematic approach ensures that the
ER model accurately represents the real-world Insurance Claim Processing workflow.
4.2  Justification of Identified Entities
PolicyHolder
The PolicyHolder entity represents customers who purchase insurance policies and sub-
mit  claims.   This  entity  is  fundamental  because  the  claim  lifecycle  originates  from  the
policyholder.
PolicyHolderID uniquely identifies each customer.  AadhaarNo is a candidate key
for verification.  Phone is a multivalued attribute.  Address is a composite attribute.
Age is derived from DOB.
PolicyHolder is modelled as a strong entity with its own primary key.
Branch
The  Branch  entity  represents  regional  offices  of  the  insurance  company  responsible  for
issuing  and managing  policies.   Branch supports  regional  reporting and  managerial ac-
countability.
BranchID uniquely identifies each branch.  Branch is modelled as a strong entity
in a 1:N relationship with Policy.
Event
The Event entity acts as a universal policy type classification mechanism, eliminating the
need for separate subclass tables.  New insurance products are introduced by adding a
new Event record only.
EventID uniquely identifies each event type.  RiskLevel supports underwriting de-
cisions.  Event is a strong entity participating in relationships with both Policy (CAT-
EGORIZES) and Claim (TRIGGERS).
Policy
The Policy entity represents insurance agreements issued to policyholders through a spe-
cific  branch,  categorised  by  an  event  type.   Claims  are  always  linked  to  a  valid  active
policy.
PolicyID is the primary key.  PolicyNumber is a candidate key.  PolicyDuration
is derived from StartDate and EndDate.  Policy is a strong entity.

ServiceProvider
The ServiceProvider entity represents third-party organisations (hospitals, vehicle repair
centres, property inspection firms) whose services are associated with claims.
ProviderID is the primary key.  LicenseNo is a candidate key ensuring regulatory
compliance.  ServiceProvider is a strong entity in a 1:N relationship with Claim.
Claim
The Claim entity is the central transactional component of the ICPS. Claims must undergo
validation, verification, and status tracking.
ClaimID  is  the  primary  key.   ClaimNumber  is  a  candidate  key.   ClaimStatus
represents workflow stages. ClaimProcessingTime is derived.  Claim carries EventID
and ProviderID as foreign key attributes.
Claim is a strong entity connected to Policy, ClaimDocument, ClaimStatusHistory,
ClaimReview, ServiceProvider, Event, and Payment.
ClaimDocument
The ClaimDocument entity represents supporting documents for claim verification.  Each
document belongs to exactly one claim and is identified by its own DocumentID as a
primary key.
ClaimDocument  is  modelled  as  a strong  entity  (updated  from  the  previous  weak
entity design to reflect that each document has its own unique identifier independent of
the composite key).
ClaimStatusHistory
The  ClaimStatusHistory  entity  represents  the  audit  trail  of  status  transitions  for  each
claim.  Each status entry is uniquely identified by its own StatusID.
ClaimStatusHistory is modelled as a strong entity (updated from the previous weak
entity design), critical for regulatory compliance and dispute resolution.
Surveyor
The Surveyor entity represents authorised personnel responsible for claim verification and
assessment. SurveyorID is the primary key.  LicenseNo is a candidate key.
Surveyor is a strong entity participating in the claim review process.
ClaimReview
ClaimReview represents the review activity performed by surveyors on claims, resolving
the many-to-many relationship between Claim and Surveyor.
ReviewID is the primary key. ClaimReview stores ReviewDate, RecommendedAmount,
and Remarks.
ClaimReview is an associative strong entity.

Payment
The  Payment  entity  represents  financial  settlement  for  approved  claims.   Payment  is

PaymentID is the primary key.  TransactionRefNo is a candidate key.  Payment is a
strong entity in a 1:1 optional relationship with Claim.
4.3  Justification of Relationships
•  A PolicyHolder owns one or more Policies (1:N). PolicyHolder — Partial; Policy
— Total.
•  A Branch manages one or more Policies (1:N). Branch — Partial; Policy — Total.
•  An Event categorises one or more Policies (1:N). Event — Partial; Policy — Total.
•  A Policy generates  multiple Claims (1:N). Policy — Partial; Claim — Total.
•  A  Claim  is  handled  by  one  ServiceProvider  (N:1).   Claim  —  Total;  Service-
Provider — Partial.
•  An Event triggers  one or more Claims (1:N). Event — Partial; Claim — Total.
•  A Claim has multiple ClaimDocuments (1:N). Both participate; ClaimDocument
— Total.
•  A Claim has  multiple ClaimStatusHistory entries (1:N). Both — Total.
•  A Claim is reviewed by  Surveyors through ClaimReview (M:N resolved).  Claim
— Partial; ClaimReview — Total.
•  A Surveyor performs  reviews through ClaimReview (1:N). Surveyor — Partial;
ClaimReview — Total.
•  A Claim  may  be settled by  one Payment  (1:1).   Claim  —  Partial;  Payment  —
Total.

7.  Relationships with Types, Implementation, and Participation
7.1  PolicyHolder – Policy (HAS)
Type:  1:N    Implementation:  Referenced (policy                                       holder id in policies)
Participation:  PolicyHolder — Partial; Policy — Total.
One policyholder owns multiple policies.  Each policy must belong to exactly one policy-
holder.
7.2  Branch – Policy (MANAGES)
Type:  1:N    Implementation:  Referenced (branch                                       id in policies)
Participation:  Branch — Partial; Policy — Total.
One branch manages multiple policies.  Each policy is issued by exactly one branch.
7.3  Event – Policy (CATEGORIZES)
Type:  1:N    Implementation:  Referenced (event                                       id in policies)
Participation:  Event — Partial; Policy — Total.
One event type categorises multiple policies.  This is the core mechanism that eliminates
subclass tables.
7.4  Policy – Claim (FILES)
Type:  1:N    Implementation:  Referenced (policy                                       id in claims)
Participation:  Policy — Partial; Claim — Total.
One policy can have multiple claims filed against it.  Each claim must reference a valid
policy.

7.5  Claim – ServiceProvider (HANDLES)
Type:  N:1    Implementation:  Referenced (provider                                       id in claims)
Participation:  Claim — Total; ServiceProvider — Partial.
Each claim is handled by one service provider. A provider may handle claims from multiple

7.6  Event – Claim (TRIGGERS)
Type:  1:N    Implementation:  Referenced (event                                       id in claims)
Participation:  Event — Partial; Claim — Total.
Each  claim  is  triggered  by  exactly  one  event  type.   This  allows  the  claim  to  carry  its
insurance category directly without joining through Policy.
7.7  Claim – ClaimDocument (HAS        DOCUMENT)
Type:  1:N    Implementation:  Embedded (documents[] array in claims)
Participation:  Claim — Partial; ClaimDocument — Total.
One claim has multiple supporting documents.  Embedded because documents are always
accessed with their claim and are bounded in size.
7.8  Claim – ClaimStatusHistory (HAS        HISTORY)
Type:  1:N    Implementation:  Embedded (status                                       history[] array in claims)
Participation:  Both — Total.
One claim has multiple status transitions forming an append-only audit trail.  Embedded
for atomic status updates.
7.9  Claim – ClaimReview (REVIEWED        BY)
Type:  1:N    Implementation:  Referenced (claim                                       id in claim     reviews)
Participation:  Claim — Partial; ClaimReview — Total.
One claim can be reviewed multiple times by different surveyors.
7.10  Surveyor – ClaimReview (PERFORMS)
Type:  1:N    Implementation:  Referenced (surveyor                                       id in claim     reviews)
Participation:  Surveyor — Partial; ClaimReview — Total.
One surveyor performs reviews on multiple claims.
7.11  Claim – Payment (SETTLED          BY)
Type:  1:1    Implementation:  Referenced (claim                                       id in payments)
Participation:  Claim — Partial; Payment — Total.
An approved claim is settled by exactly one payment.  Not all claims result in payment.

12.  Conclusion
The redesigned NoSQL document-based schema for the Insurance Claim Processing Sys-
tem (ICPS) successfully delivers the following outcomes:
•  All  Strong  Entities:   The  updated  ER  design  models  all  entities  —  including
ClaimDocument  and  ClaimStatusHistory  —  as  strong  entities  with  independent
primary keys.  In the NoSQL implementation, these are embedded for performance
while retaining their identifiers within the array.
•  TRIGGERS  Relationship:   The  Event  entity  participates  in  both  CATEGO-
RIZES (Policy) and TRIGGERS (Claim), allowing the insurance product type to
be accessed directly from the Claim document via event                                          id without joining through
Policy.
•  Scalability:  Horizontal sharding via claims.                                  id handles high claim volumes.
•  Flexibility: The Event-based classification supports all six insurance product types
without schema migration.
•  Performance:   Embedding  ClaimDocuments  and  StatusHistory  enables  single-
query claim lifecycle retrieval.
•  Data Integrity:  Primary keys, unique indexes, and reference IDs maintain consis-
tency across all nine collections.
•  Extensibility: New products, branches, and providers are added as new documents
only.
